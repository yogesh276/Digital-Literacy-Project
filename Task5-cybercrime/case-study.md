# Case Study: UPI Fraud

UPI fraud has become one of the fastest-growing cybercrimes in India due to the widespread use of digital payment platforms such as Google Pay, PhonePe, and Paytm. It involves deceiving users into authorizing transactions or revealing sensitive information like OTPs and UPI PINs.

A common method used by fraudsters begins with a phone call, SMS, or message on social media where they pretend to be bank officials, customer support agents, or even buyers/sellers from online marketplaces. They often create urgency by claiming that the user’s account is at risk, a refund is pending, or a payment needs to be verified immediately.

The fraudster then sends a “collect request” (payment request) to the victim’s UPI app and instructs them to approve it, falsely claiming that doing so will result in money being credited to their account. In reality, once the victim enters their UPI PIN, the money is debited from their account. In some cases, scammers also send fake links that mimic official banking websites or ask users to download malicious applications that steal personal and financial data.

Students, elderly individuals, and people unfamiliar with digital payments are especially vulnerable to such scams. The consequences can be severe, including financial loss, identity theft, and emotional distress. In many cases, victims are unable to recover their money if the fraud is not reported quickly.

This case study highlights the importance of digital literacy and awareness. Users must understand that no legitimate bank or service provider will ever ask for sensitive details like OTPs or UPI PINs. Being cautious, verifying sources, and avoiding suspicious requests are essential steps in preventing such cybercrimes.
